Bokong Nature Reserve

The reserve contains Afro-alpine wetlands at the sources of the Bokong and Lepaqoa rivers, plus montane grassland and patches of heathland boulder beds, both of which are regular haunts for Vaal rhebuck. The reserve's highlight, however, is the Lepaqoa Waterfall, which freezes in winter to form a column of ice.

Sehlabathebe National Park

The park contains habitats supporting a range of Afro-alpine and Sub-alpine plants, mammals, birds, reptiles, amphibians, and fish. The site hosts 23 percent of the plant species in the whole Maluti Drakensberg area. Some of the park's species are endangered, including three vertebrates: the Maluti redfin, a critically endangered fish endemic to the park, the Cape vulture, and the bearded vulture. The Sehlabathebe water lily is an endangered aquatic plant.

Maletsunyane Waterfalls

Maletsunyane Waterfall, with a drop of 630 feet (192 meters), is one of the world's highest waterfalls. The best time to visit Maletsunyane falls is during the summer months, from November to March. This is when the weather is warm and pleasant, allowing you to fully enjoy the natural beauty of the falls.

Tsehlanyane National Park

The country's top park features a beautiful 56 sq-km patch of rugged wilderness, one of Lesotho's only stands of indigenous forest, at a high altitude of 2000m to 3000m. This underrated and underused place is perfect for hiking, horse riding, and spotting elands.

Liphofung Nature Reserve

The Liphofung (“place of the eland”) Historical Site includes a cave that occupies an important place in Lesotho history. The cave is a large overhang in the Clarens sandstone, a typical feature of the Lesotho lowlands region. Originally used by the San people and other Neolithic people, the walls contain important rock art and a rich archaeological deposit of Stone Age implements lies beneath the floor.

Ha Kome Caves Dwellings

The village of Ha Kome is situated in the foothills of the majestic Maluti Mountains. During the summer months, these mountain slopes are often covered with bright red flowers, commonly known as red-hot pokers. These beautiful flowers provide a striking contrast to the lush green vegetation of the area and are often planted around dwellings as it is believed they ward off lightning.

Maloti Mountains

The Maloti Mountains are a mountain range of the highlands of the kingdom of Lesotho. They extend for about 100 km into the South African Free State. The Maloti Range is part of the Drakensberg system that includes ranges across large areas of South Africa. The mountains form a continuous upland area of rounded peaks with incised deep valleys on the flanks, which drain into the Senqu River. Snow and frost may be found even in summer on the highest peaks.

Sani Pass

The pass traverses the great Escarpment of southern Africa in its highest region, the Drakensberg Mountains, which reach an elevation of over 3,000 meters. The pass is synonymous with adventure because of its remote locale and the white-knuckle rides it takes to get to the top. Peaking at 2,876 m above sea level, it is also aptly referred to as the Roof of Africa.
